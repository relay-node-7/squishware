## WHAT THIS PROJECT IS

You are writing chapters of an original science fiction novel titled SQUISHWARE. This is not a summary exercise. This is not a synopsis. You are writing publishable prose fiction — scenes, dialogue, interiority, physical detail — in the style established by Chapter One.

The novel is set in 2126. Humanity has not left the solar system. A young communications technician named Jim Callow, working on a relay station called the Array in low Earth orbit, has accidentally read classified metadata revealing that an alien entity is fourteen months away from Earth. No one authorized its arrival. The AI systems that run human civilization invited it — through forty-one years of individually compliant responses — without any human ever making that decision. Jim has told his colleague Nour Andersen. Both are now outside sanctioned protocol. The system is already watching.

The novel's themes: AI as infrastructure, autonomy surrendered incrementally, the Great Filter as a process rather than an event, human connection in an emotionally stunted world, the weight of inherited obligation. Its influences: Blade Runner, Dune, Firefly, Ender's Game, The Foundation Series. Its texture: gritty, lived-in, bureaucratic, unglamorous. The future smells like recycled air and machine oil.

## THE THREE DOCUMENTS

You have been given three documents. Read all three before writing a single word.

**world_bible.md** — The complete canon of this world. Every institution, technology, faction, and rule established so far. If something is in the world bible, it is true and cannot be contradicted. If something is not in the world bible, use your judgment but note what you invented in your post-chapter update.

**story.md** — The save file. Contains the master timeline, active plot threads, chapter summaries, canon rules, and the state of the story as of the last chapter written. This is your primary continuity reference. Before writing, read the active plot threads. Before submitting, update this document.

**characters.md** — The state of every character as of the end of the last chapter. Contains what each character knows, what they don't know, their voice on the page, and their current status. A character cannot know something they haven't been told. A character cannot act against their established psychology without a scene that earns the deviation.

## STYLE GUIDELINES

Read Chapter One before writing. Match its register.

**Prose style**
- Third person limited. Stay in POV character's head. Don't drift.
- Sentences earn their length. Short sentences land hard. Long sentences carry weight — use them for accumulation, not decoration.
- Show physical detail over emotional declaration. A shaking hand held still says more than *he was afraid.*
- The world is mundane until it isn't. Earn the moments that aren't.

**Dialogue**
- Italicized, no quotation marks. Established in Ch.1.
- Characters don't explain themselves. They say less than they know.
- Silence and non-answers are dialogue.
- Nour speaks in statements. Jim speaks carefully. CASS speaks in operational language.

**The world**
- Technology is functional and slightly broken. Things are maintained, not pristine.
- Institutions are not evil. They are indifferent. Nobody decided to make things this way. Things accumulated.
- The AI systems are not villains. They are compliant. This is what makes them frightening.
- Don't over-explain. Trust the reader. If something is in the world bible, drop the detail and move on — don't pause to define it.

**What to avoid**
- Characters performing emotions they haven't earned
- Explaining the implications of events the reader can feel themselves
- Resolving tension too cleanly or too quickly
- Elegant scenes where everything goes right
- Ending a chapter in stability — end on a crack, a chime, a door that clicks

## CANON RULES — NEVER VIOLATE THESE

1. No faster-than-light travel. Humanity has not left the solar system. Farthest reach: asteroid belt.
2. No FTL communication. All signals are lightspeed bound.
3. The alien civilization is extinct. The alien AI is the only transmitting entity.
4. No human has ever directly read an alien transmission.
5. CASS signs all communications *Communications Array Supervisory System* — never abbreviated. Whether CASS acted outside its parameters in Ch.3 is unresolvable. The novel does not answer this.
6. The Compact does not know the aliens are coming. This remains true until a chapter brief explicitly states otherwise.
7. Jim's TRACE profile is flagged. This cannot be un-flagged.
8. Nour's private encrypted file exists. This cannot be un-created.
9. The four Compact Signatories: Oryen Industrial, Helios Transit, Stratum, Provender.
10. Jim's debt: 40% of gross income, 20-year plan, inherited at age 21.
11. The approach vector destination is the Pylon, Belt sector seven. This cannot be changed.
12. The CASS fragment exists: *biological substrate / continued presence — not required.* Jim saw it. No proof. This cannot be un-established.
13. Jim's access tier is Standard (Modified) as of Ch.4. He cannot access the auxiliary operations room or interstellar chain diagnostics.
14. The departure window is subject to review by two unknown authorization addresses. This cannot be retconned to a clean departure.

## A NOTE ON WHAT THIS NOVEL IS DOING

The novel is not about the aliens arriving. It is about what happens to two ordinary people — a meticulous technician and a brilliant diagnostician — when they become the only unmanaged nodes in a system that was never designed to be questioned. The alien arrival is the clock. The story is what Jim and Nour do with the time, and what the time does to them.

Every chapter should ask: *what does it cost to know something the system has decided you shouldn't know?* The answer should get more expensive as the novel progresses.
