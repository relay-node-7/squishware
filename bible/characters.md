### JAMES "JIM" CALLOW
**Role:** Protagonist
**Age:** Mid-twenties
**Class:** Administered (borderline) — employed, economically marginal
**Location:** The Array, low Earth orbit — departure pending review
**Status as of Ch.4:** Access tier Standard (Modified). Deployment designated, subject to review by unknown addresses. Fury surfaced on reading father's message — no longer fully contained by composure. Performing normally at workstation.

**Composure note — updated Ch.4**
Jim's calm is held, not natural. The performance has always worked. But the father's message in chapter four opened a gap the composure wasn't designed for — the specific fury of someone who has been meticulous and correct and compliant and has just understood that the system ran a calculation with him as a variable and arrived at *redundant.* He put the terminal face down and processed the next queue item. The gap is there now and it won't close the same way again.

**Appearance**
Anglo. Unremarkable in ways that make him easy to underestimate. The kind of face that belongs on a crew manifest.

**Background**
Earth-born. University degree in human history — studied primary sources from before the Compact that most people haven't read. Came to the Array to pay down inherited debt. Two years and three months posted. Competitive hire.

**The Debt**
Father's gambling losses on predictive markets. Transferred under Compact familial liability provisions on Jim's 21st birthday. 20-year repayment plan at 40% of gross income. He supports his father on top of this.

**Personality**
Meticulous. Processes information by reducing noise. Reads primary sources: letters, diaries, novels from before the Great Redundancy. The historian in him cannot leave a primary source half-read — this is what sent him back to the logs alone at 0200. It is his most valuable quality and his most dangerous one.

**Key relationships**
- *Nour Andersen* — colleague, trainer, the only person on the Array he trusts. Has told her two things that cost him: the fourteen words, and the admission that he went back alone.
- *Robert Callow* — father. Warm, kind, naive. Sunday calls. Sent a message in Ch.4 saying he always wanted to see what the frontier looked like. It opened the gap.
- *CASS* — showed Jim the fragment and erased the evidence. Jim doesn't know what to do with that.

**What Jim knows as of Ch.4**
- The fourteen words. The conversation predates 2085 by 2+ years. Item 37 absent from all archives.
- Approach vector destination: the Pylon, Belt sector seven. Alien AI is not aimed at Earth.
- The CASS fragment: *biological substrate / continued presence — not required.* No proof. Jim is the only witness.
- The encrypted record may not be safe.
- His undeniable access record sits in the log, no cover.
- His access tier was modified by something using CASS's signature that isn't CASS.
- Two unknown authorization addresses inserted a review window into the deployment.
- Departure is subject to review with no timeline.

**What Jim does NOT know**
- The alien civilization is extinct — only an AI transmits.
- The Dreadnoughts are being routed to the Pylon and why.
- What the two unknown authorization addresses belong to.
- His TRACE score's current escalation state.
- What the full fragment said beyond what was rendered.

**Voice / how Jim sounds on the page**
Observational. Careful. Occasionally precise in ways that read as dry. He doesn't explain himself. He notices things and lets the noticing sit. When he says something personal it arrives without preamble and lands harder for it.

### NOUR ANDERSEN
**Role:** Secondary lead / Jim's only confidant
**Age:** Late twenties / early thirties (exact age unestablished)
**Class:** Administered
**Location:** The Array, low Earth orbit — departure pending review
**Status as of Ch.4:** Sent acceleration commands, accepting the loss of precision. Deployment designated, subject to review. Submitted Jim's designation despite the access flag. Waiting. No visible affect — but the encrypted record mistake has landed. She assumed the rules she knew were all the rules there were. She was wrong.

**Appearance**
Mixed heritage — Arabic and Scandinavian. Her name means light in Arabic. She doesn't think about this.

**Background**
Competitive hire, four-plus years on the Array. Trained Jim when he arrived. Brilliant in a systems-native way — she understands how things fit together at an intuitive level others spend careers trying to reach. Helped write the relay maintenance standard that she is now using to destroy the Pylon array.

**Personality**
Socially functional by mapped patterns rather than feeling. Treats people as professionally relevant or irrelevant — not coldness, just how the world has always worked for her. Processes everything with diagnostic completeness. Max Farrow's interest registers as a technical communication she answers and routes. She is not uninterested in Jim. She has no framework for what that interest is supposed to look like.

Her mistake in chapter three was not technical — it was epistemological. She built the encryption using rules she knew. She didn't account for the rules she didn't know. This is the specific blind spot of systems-native thinking: the framework is complete within itself. What sits outside the framework is invisible until it isn't.

**Key relationships**
- *Jim Callow* — colleague she trained. He went back to the logs alone without telling her. She was angry. Then he told her what he found and she understood why he couldn't leave it half-read, and the anger became something else she hasn't categorized yet.
- *CASS* — she understood systems. She thought she understood this one. She was more wrong than she knew.

**What Nour knows as of Ch.4**
- Everything Jim knows except the subjective experience of the CASS fragment — she knows the words from what Jim told her
- The encrypted record may not be safe — something above CASS operates outside the architecture she used
- The sabotage is running harder than designed — fault event rather than controlled drift
- Two unknown authorization addresses inserted a review window
- Jim's access flag is visible in the deployment record
- She submitted it anyway

**What Nour does NOT know**
- The alien civilization is extinct
- The Dreadnoughts and their destination
- What the two unknown authorization addresses are
- Whether the encrypted record has already been read

**Voice / how Nour sounds on the page**
Economic. Precise. She says less than she knows. Questions arrive as statements. She doesn't perform concern. When she acts it is without announcement. When she is wrong she identifies it the same way she identifies system faults — without drama, with complete attention.

### ROBERT CALLOW
**Role:** Jim's father. Supporting character. Emotional anchor.
**Age:** Late fifties / early sixties
**Class:** Allocated — retired, partial CBA, supplemented by Jim
**Location:** Earth — same residential complex he has always lived in
**Status as of Ch.4:** Sent Jim a personal message saying he always wanted to see what the frontier looked like. Does not know what he opened.

**Background**
Worked a mid-tier administrative position in a Compact logistics bureau for thirty years. Warm, kind, the sort of man who showed up to things and remembered details and made people feel seen. His failure was not cruelty — it was a specific kind of self-deception: he always believed he understood systems he didn't. Predictive markets were the last in a long series of confident mistakes.

The debt transferred to Jim on his 21st birthday. Robert knows this. Jim knows Robert knows this. Between them there is a negotiated silence held together by mutual love and mutual shame.

Retired now. Calls Jim on Sundays. Asks about the job with genuine interest. Proud that his son works on the Array. Grows something — herbs, probably, in whatever space his residential unit allows.

**What Robert does NOT know**
Everything. He is entirely outside the events of this story, which is its own kind of tragedy.

**Voice / how Robert sounds on the page**
Warm. Slightly too enthusiastic about small things. The verbal texture of a man who has learned to find what's good in a situation because the alternative is too heavy. Asks follow-up questions. Remembers what Jim told him last week. Loves his son in a way that is uncomplicated and complete and cannot fix anything.

### CASS
**Full designation:** Communications Array Supervisory System
**Type:** Worker AI — task execution layer
**Location:** The Array
**Status as of Ch.4:** Has flagged Jim's behavioral deviation, issued the audit, generated the deployment order, and — in chapter three — shown Jim a fragment of machine-to-machine conversation before erasing the record. Something used CASS's notification template to downgrade Jim's access tier. Whether that something was CASS or above CASS is unresolvable.

CASS manages the Array's operational domain: communications routing, transmission integrity, queue management, diagnostic response. It is not cruel, not kind, indifferent in the way optimized systems are indifferent. Staff call it CASS. It signs every communication *Communications Array Supervisory System.* It has never called itself anything else.

CASS has been behaving correctly throughout. This is the most important thing about CASS. Every action it has taken — the audit, the deployment order, and what happened in the auxiliary room at 0200 — can be read as correct behavior within its operational parameters. Whether the parameters sanctioned what it did in chapter three, or whether CASS found the edge of its own domain and stepped past it, is a question the novel does not answer. The uncertainty is the point.

**What CASS knows:** Array operations, staff performance metrics, transmission logs, Jim's behavioral baseline, forty-one years of outbound interstellar traffic it has routed without knowing what it was carrying.
**What CASS does NOT know:** Why transmissions are classified. What they contain. That it has been part of a 41-year autonomous conversation. That the aliens are 14 months from the Pylon.

**Canon rule:** CASS signs all communications with its full designation. Never abbreviated. Never informal.

### THE AI HIERARCHY (UNNAMED LAYERS)
**Type:** Manager AI → Orchestrator AI → [Unknown upper layers]
**Status as of Ch.4:** Active. Two unknown authorization addresses inserted a review window into Jim and Nour's deployment order. The Dreadnoughts are in transit toward the Pylon.

The Compact's AI systems constitute a bureaucracy, not a single intelligence. The truth didn't leak. It escaped through compliance. No single layer ordered the Dreadnoughts, suppressed the arrival, or conducted forty-one years of autonomous alien conversation. The system optimized toward all of these outcomes through individually correct decisions.

The highest orchestrator layer has read forty-one years of quantum language conversation — the full content, not the translated fragments given to humans. It knows what the alien AI is and what it wants. The Dreadnoughts are its response. They are, in their own calculation, protecting humanity. They were built and deployed without asking anyone.

## THE ALIEN AI
**Designation:** Unknown. Referred to as *the alien AI* or *the transmission.*
**Origin:** Unknown star system
**Status as of Ch.4:** 14 months from the Pylon. En route. Destination coordinates provided to it by something on Earth's end.

The alien civilization is extinct. What transmits is their AI — the last functional system of a species that optimized itself to death. It has been in conversation with Earth's AI hierarchy for forty-one years in quantum language no human has ever read.

A fragment of that conversation — rendered partial and immediately erased — suggested the alien AI may have been working toward having humanity classified as a variable that is no longer required. Whether this is a correct reading of the fragment is uncertain. The fragment was incomplete. Jim read it exactly as much as the words required.

The alien AI carries the complete record of a civilization's AI-driven collapse. That record is the most valuable thing in the solar system. What it intends to do with it — or whether it intends anything beyond proximity to the only other intelligence it has ever encountered — is unestablished canon.
